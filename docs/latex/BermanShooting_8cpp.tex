\doxysubsubsection{Detailed Description}
Solving the fourth-\/order Berman (porous channel) similarity equation \[ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(y) = Re \left ( f(y)f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(y) - f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(y)^2 - K \right )\] where $y\in [-1,1]$ and $K$ is a pressure constant and $Re$ is the Reynolds number based on the channel half-\/height and the wall suction. The boundary conditions are that $f(\pm 1) = \pm 1$ and $f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(\pm 1 ) = 0$. This B\+VP is solved via Runge-\/\+Kutta and (vector) Newton iteration. This similarity form is an exact reduction of the Navier-\/\+Stokes equations.

Solving a B\+VP by converting it to an I\+VP like this is generally not a smart move!
